Smooth Functions

Definition

A differentiable function f(x)f(x)f(x) is called smooth iff it has a Lipschitz continuous gradient, i.e. iff L<+∞L<+\infinL<+∞ such that

∥∇f(x)−∇f(z)∥2≤L∥x−z∥2,∀x,z∈Rn\|\nabla f(\boldsymbol{x})-\nabla f(\boldsymbol{z})\|_{2} \leq L\|\boldsymbol{x}-\boldsymbol{z}\|_{2}, \quad \forall \boldsymbol{x}, \boldsymbol{z} \in \mathbb{R}^{n} ∥∇f(x)−∇f(z)∥2​≤L∥x−z∥2​,∀x,z∈Rn

Note: Lipschitz continuity of ∇f\nabla f∇f is a stronger condition than mere continuity, so any differentiable function whose gradient is Lipschitz continuous is a continuously differentiable function.
