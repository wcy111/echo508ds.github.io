Vector Norm

Definition

A vector norm is any real-valued function ∥⋅∥\|\cdot\|∥⋅∥ that satisfies the following properties

  • if x≠0x \neq 0x​=0 then ∥x∥≥0\|x\|\geq0∥x∥≥0

  • triangle inequality: ∥x+y∥≤∥x∥+∥y∥\|\boldsymbol{x}+\boldsymbol{y}\| \leq\|\boldsymbol{x}\|+\|\boldsymbol{y}\|∥x+y∥≤∥x∥+∥y∥

It can be valid, ∥x∥p=(∑i∣xi∣p)1/p\|\boldsymbol{x}\|_{p}=\left(\sum_{i}\left|x_{i}\right|^{p}\right)^{1 / p}∥x∥p​=(∑i​∣xi​∣p)1/p, for any p≥1p \geq 1p≥1

∥x∥1=∑i∣xi∣∥x∥2=∑ixi2∥x∥∞=max⁡i∣xi∣.\begin{aligned} &\|\boldsymbol{x}\|_{1}=\sum_{i}\left|x_{i}\right| \\ &\|\boldsymbol{x}\|_{2}=\sqrt{\sum_{i} x_{i}^{2}} \\ &\|\boldsymbol{x}\|_{\infty}=\max _{i}\left|x_{i}\right| . \end{aligned} ​∥x∥1​=i∑​∣xi​∣∥x∥2​=i∑​xi2​​∥x∥∞​=imax​∣xi​∣.​

Unitary invariant matrix norms

Let A∈Rm×n\boldsymbol{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}A∈Rm×n. We say the matrix norm is unitary invariant if

∥A∥♯=∥PAQ∥♯,∀P∈O(m),Q∈O(n).\|\boldsymbol{A}\|_{\sharp}=\|\boldsymbol{P} \boldsymbol{A} \boldsymbol{Q}\|_{\sharp}, \quad \forall \boldsymbol{P} \in O(m), \boldsymbol{Q} \in O(n) . ∥A∥♯​=∥PAQ∥♯​,∀P∈O(m),Q∈O(n).

Note In linear algebra , a complex U is unitary if its conjugate transpose U is also its inverse.

  • Spectral norm. ∥A∥2→2=σ1(A)=∥σ(A)∥∞\|\boldsymbol{A}\|_{2 \rightarrow 2}=\sigma_{1}(\boldsymbol{A})=\|\boldsymbol{\sigma}(\boldsymbol{A})\|_{\infty}∥A∥2→2​=σ1​(A)=∥σ(A)∥∞​

  • Frobenius norm.∥A∥F=∑i=1min⁡{m,n}σi2(A)=∥σ(A)∥2\|\boldsymbol{A}\|_{F}=\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{\min \{m, n\}} \sigma_{i}^{2}(\boldsymbol{A})}=\|\boldsymbol{\sigma}(\boldsymbol{A})\|_{2}∥A∥F​=∑i=1min{m,n}​σi2​(A)​=∥σ(A)∥2​

    - Furthermore frobenius norm can be written as:

∥X∥F=tr⁡(X∗X)=∑i=1m∑j=1n∣Xij∣2\|\boldsymbol{X}\|_{F}=\sqrt{\operatorname{tr}\left(\boldsymbol{X}^{*} \boldsymbol{X}\right)}=\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{j=1}^{n}\left|X_{i j}\right|^{2}} ∥X∥F​=tr(X∗X)​=i=1∑m​j=1∑n​∣Xij​∣2​

Spectral norm and Frobenius norm are all Unitary invariant.
